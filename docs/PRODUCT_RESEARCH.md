# NEST — Product & Competitive Research

**Prepared:** 2026-07-18 · **Scope:** friendship, community, event, and student-social products relevant to NEST (women-only friendship app for international students in Madrid).
**Method:** live web research on 2026-07-18 (official sites, app-store listings, help centers, tech press, review platforms). Every product referenced was checked for current existence; anything that could not be verified as alive is flagged.

**Label key — used throughout:**
- **[FACT]** — verified against a cited source on the access date.
- **[OPINION]** — user reviews / anecdotal feedback; not representative data.
- **[INFERENCE]** — our design interpretation; not stated by the source.
- **[NEST]** — recommendation for NEST derived from the above.

---

## 1. Executive summary

1. The friendship-app category consolidated hard in 2024–2025: Hey! VINA shut down, Geneva was absorbed into Bumble's new BFF app, and the survivors (Timeleft, Pie, 222) are all **event-first, not swipe-first**.
2. The winning pattern of 2025–2026 is **"curated small group, fixed time, real venue"** — the app's job is to remove the scheduling and social-risk burden, not to maximize chat.
3. The most repeated failure mode is NEST's biggest risk: **match → chat → fizzle** (ghosting, three-message conversations, swipe fatigue). Products that force a concrete plan early avoid it.
4. **Safety became a visible product surface** in 2025: ID/selfie verification badges, date-sharing with trusted contacts, pre-send message screening. NEST's manual admin verification is a genuine differentiator worth surfacing loudly.
5. Madrid already has strong offline student infrastructure (ESN sections, Citylife Madrid, Girl Gone International). NEST should complement it (women-only, curated, small) rather than compete on event volume.

---

## 2. Market context (why now)

- **[FACT]** Dating-app burnout is measured and mainstream: a Forbes Health survey reported roughly 78–79% of Gen Z users experiencing "dating app burnout." (forbes.com/health/dating/dating-app-fatigue/, accessed 2026-07-18.)
- **[FACT]** Major dating platforms are pivoting to in-person events as Gen Z engagement declines (CNBC, 2025-03-26: "Dating apps are dialing up in-person events…", accessed 2026-07-18). Bumble cut ~30% of staff in mid-2025 amid declining revenue (reported across tech press).
- **[FACT]** Bumble's stated strategic bet is friendship: it launched a rebuilt standalone BFF app in September 2025, with CEO Whitney Wolfe Herd saying "what people really crave is belonging" (Fast Company, 2025-09-18).
- **[INFERENCE]** The category is converging on: small groups, curated matching, real-world anchors, visible safety. Pure 1:1 swipe-to-chat friendship apps are being abandoned by their own makers.
- **[NEST]** NEST's existing stack (curated events + discovery + verified members) is already closer to the 2026 winning shape than a swipe app; the roadmap should lean into events and group formats, not deepen the swipe loop.

## 3. Product deep dives

### 3.1 Bumble BFF (new standalone app, 2025) — ALIVE (US only at launch)

**Status [FACT]:** Launched 2025-09-18 as a rebuilt standalone friendship app for US users, replacing the 2023 "Bumble For Friends" app in the US; the older app reportedly remained available internationally. Built on technology from Geneva (acquired 2024).
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://www.fastcompany.com/91406463/bumble-launches-bff-as-a-standalone-app-to-lead-the-great-frienaissance (pub. 2025-09-18)
- https://techcrunch.com/2025/09/18/bumble-bffs-revamped-app-is-here-focusing-on-friend-groups-and-community-building/ (pub. 2025-09-18)
- https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20230726935186/en/ (pub. 2023-07-26, original BFF app launch)

**Observed features:**
- **[FACT]** Interest-based matching, photo prompts, customizable profiles, profile verification.
- **[FACT]** "Groups" area powered by Geneva: interest-based group spaces plus local event discovery — friendship framed as 1:1 *and* group-based from day one.
- **[FACT]** Bumble-commissioned research cited at launch: 55% of 18–35-year-olds want more local friends.
- **[OPINION]** Long-running complaints about the previous BFF experience (review blogs, Reddit threads summarized by third parties): heavy ghosting; "lots of matches, few friendships"; conversations fizzle after ~3 exchanges; the photo-first swipe format feels like dating; the 24-hour first-message timer adds dating-style pressure. (e.g. introvrs.com/blog/bumble-bff-review, whistleout.com guide — anecdotal, some authored by competitors; treat as directional only.)

**Why it matters:** the biggest player concluded that 1:1 swipe friendship fails without groups and events, and rebuilt its app around them.
**Limitation/risk:** still photo-first at its core; US-only at launch, so its presence for Madrid students in 2026 is unverified.
**[NEST]** Do not replicate the swipe-and-chat core deeper; replicate the *lesson*: pair every 1:1 connection with a group or event context, and remove countdown-style pressure mechanics.

### 3.2 Geneva — ACQUIRED & SHUT DOWN (absorbed into BFF)

**Status [FACT]:** Group/community chat app acquired by Bumble (agreement announced May 2024). The standalone Geneva app was shut down in 2025; members were migrated into the new BFF app's Groups.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/20/bumble-buys-community-building-app-geneva-to-expand-further-into-friendships/ (pub. 2024-05-20)
- https://www.geneva.com/blog/meant-to-bee (Geneva's own acquisition announcement)

**Observed pattern:** **[FACT]** Geneva's model was interest-based groups ("clubs") with chat spaces and event coordination, popular with run clubs and women-led communities; Bumble bought it explicitly to expand friendship from 1:1 to groups.
**Why it matters:** validates small-group spaces as the retention engine for friendship products.
**Limitation/risk:** standalone community apps struggled to monetize (Geneva reportedly generated no revenue before shutdown); community features carry heavy moderation load.
**[NEST]** Groups are strategically right but expensive to operate safely. For NEST they belong in the roadmap as an admin-moderated, capped, approval-gated feature — not a general-purpose group chat.

### 3.3 Hey! VINA — DEFUNCT (do not benchmark as live)

**Status [FACT with caveat]:** Multiple third-party sources report Hey! VINA announced its shutdown around late 2024 citing inability to maintain the product (e.g. gofrendly.com blog, pub. 2024-12-26 — note: written by a competitor). Its marketing site (heyvina.com) may still resolve, but **we could not verify any active operation in 2026**. Treat as dead.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):** https://www.gofrendly.com/blog/2024/12/26/hey-vina-is-shutting-down-the-best-alternatives-for-women-to-make-new-friends/ ; https://www.heyvina.com/
**Historical pattern worth keeping:** **[FACT]** VINA was the pioneering women-only friendship app (swipe-based, quizzes, communities).
**Why it matters:** proof that "women-only Tinder-for-friends" alone is not a durable product, even with a strong brand and press.
**[NEST]** Women-only is necessary but not sufficient. Differentiation must come from the Madrid/student context, curation, and real-world outcomes — not from the matching mechanic itself.

### 3.4 Timeleft — ALIVE, ACTIVE IN MADRID, women-only format exists

**Status [FACT]:** Active in 2026; claims 200+ cities in ~50+ countries and 3M+ guests seated. Present in Madrid since early 2024. Runs **women-only dinner tables in select cities** ("Meet 5 Women Over Dinner"), including Spanish cities.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://timeleft.com/ and https://timeleft.com/locations/
- https://timeleft.com/blog/2023/12/29/timelefts-madrid-fiesta-sharing-meals-in-the-city-of-movida/ (pub. 2023-12-29)
- https://timeleft.com/post/meet-5-women-over-dinner/
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/timeleft-make-new-friends-irl/id6466442949

**Observed features:**
- **[FACT]** Personality quiz → algorithmic seating of 5–6 strangers; fixed weekly ritual (Wednesday dinners; women-only tables on other nights in some cities); restaurant revealed only the morning of the dinner; subscription covers matching + reservation; in-app icebreaker/conversation cards at the table; post-dinner formats expanding (drinks, coffee, runs).
- **[OPINION]** Trustpilot complaints (anecdotal): restaurants not matching the chosen budget tier; no-shows — including a member seated alone; billing/refund friction; group chat access closed after events; slow support. (trustpilot.com/review/timeleft.com, accessed 2026-07-18.)

**Why it matters:** the cleanest demonstration that removing choice (fixed day, curated table, hidden venue) removes friction — users show up without ever "chatting first."
**Limitation/risk:** operational quality (venues, no-shows, refunds) is the whole product; when logistics fail, trust collapses. Cost-sensitive students are exposed to budget mismatch.
**[NEST]** A weekly, admin-curated small-table ritual for 4–6 members is the single most proven concept NEST could adapt — and NEST can pilot it with its existing events + RSVP system. Budget transparency and a no-show policy must be designed in from day one.

### 3.5 Pie — ALIVE (US cities)

**Status [FACT]:** Active; founded by Bonobos co-founder Andy Dunn; operating in Chicago, Austin, and the SF Bay Area as of 2025; raised $11.5M.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/04/andy-dunns-new-app-pie-uses-ai-to-help-you-make-friends/ (pub. 2025-03-04)
- https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/19/irl-social-app-pie-is-coming-to-sf-to-make-you-less-lonely/ (pub. 2024-11-19)
- https://getpie.app/

**Observed features:**
- **[FACT]** Short personality quiz → assignment to groups of ~6 for a local activity; a **group chat opens automatically before the meetup with icebreakers**, so nobody has to send the first message; philosophy explicitly based on repeated exposure to the same people (citing Marisa Franco's *Platonic*).
- **[FACT]** Press-reported engagement: ~250k people viewing plans monthly, ~50k joining (company-reported figures via Fox Business/TechCrunch; treat as marketing numbers).

**Why it matters:** solves the cold-start message problem structurally — the system opens the conversation, the humans just join it.
**Limitation/risk:** AI-assigned groups can misfire; small-city liquidity is hard; US-only.
**[NEST]** Adopt the principle "no one should ever have to invent an opener": pre-seeded conversation context around a concrete plan. Also adopt *repetition* — reunite the same people more than once.

### 3.6 222 — ALIVE (17 cities)

**Status [FACT]:** Active; curated-experience app matching strangers via a long personality questionnaire into dinners of ~6, then merging tables at a second venue later the same evening; 17 cities; venue-partnership business model.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://www.fastcompany.com/91356813/222-aims-to-end-loneliness-by-engineering-chance
- https://partners.222.place/
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/222-find-your-people/id6450612690

**Observed pattern:** **[FACT]** Two-stage evenings (intimate dinner → larger mixer) engineer both depth and breadth in one night. Revenue flows partly through venue partners.
**[OPINION]** Independent reviews describe uneven group chemistry and variable venue quality (e.g. dishamahajan.substack.com review — anecdotal).
**Why it matters / [NEST]:** the "small table first, bigger room after" shape maps neatly onto NEST events: a 6-person pre-event coffee that flows into the main admin event lowers the intimidation of walking into a big room alone.

### 3.7 Peanut — ALIVE (adjacent: women-only, life-stage based)

**Status [FACT]:** Active women-only social app (motherhood, fertility, menopause life stages).
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/peanut-find-mom-friends/id1178656034
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peanut_App
- https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.teampeanut.peanut

**Observed features:**
- **[FACT]** **Selfie verification for all profiles**; women-only admission; zero-tolerance conduct policy; groups and discussion threads; "Pods" live-audio rooms with raise-hand speaking and host moderation; a light "wave"-style connect gesture instead of hot-or-not swiping.
**Why it matters:** the strongest mainstream example of women-only trust mechanics: verify everyone, moderate visibly, soften the connect gesture.
**Limitation/risk:** life-stage communities self-segment; heavy community surface requires standing moderation staff/tooling.
**[NEST]** NEST's manual admin verification already exceeds selfie-only checks — surface it as a visible badge and a first-class brand promise. Peanut also shows that connect gestures can be warm ("wave") rather than judgment-flavored (swipe left/right).

### 3.8 Meetup — ALIVE (under Bending Spoons; organizer trust eroding)

**Status [FACT]:** Active; acquired by Bending Spoons (January 2024). Official blog confirms pricing/product restructuring.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://www.meetup.com/blog/recent-changes-and-a-look-toward-the-future/ (official)
- https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=38953242 (acquisition discussion, 2024-01)
- https://andypiper.co.uk/2024/10/18/meetup-com-is-so-over/ (pub. 2024-10-18, personal essay)
- https://medium.com/@hoffbits/pay-to-skip-why-meetup-risks-undermining-its-own-community-635cb1c25f9c

**Observed pattern:**
- **[FACT]** Core mechanics remain the reference for interest groups: recurring groups, event pages, RSVP with capacity + waitlists, member questions, attendance history.
- **[OPINION]** Widely reported organizer anger (anecdotal but voluminous): organizer fees rising sharply, formerly free features paywalled, a "pay to skip the waitlist" subscription perk at free events perceived as betraying community norms, deliverability/support problems.
**Why it matters:** demonstrates both the durable event mechanics (capacity, waitlist, recurrence) and how quickly monetizing access *inside* community events destroys goodwill.
**[NEST]** Copy the mechanics (capacity, waitlist, recurring series). Never sell queue-jumping or gate core belonging behind Premium; monetize convenience or extras instead.

### 3.9 Partiful — ALIVE (event invites; US-centric)

**Status [FACT]:** Active; free event-invite platform; Google Play "Best App of 2024"; TIME100 Most Influential Companies 2025.
**Sources (accessed 2026-07-18):**
- https://partiful.com/ and https://help.partiful.com/hc/en-us/articles/26526377667739-Why-use-Partiful
- https://party.pro/partiful/ (independent review/guide)

**Observed features:**
- **[FACT]** RSVP requires no app install (one link for everyone); guest list visible after RSVP; host "Text Blasts" + automatic reminders; post-event shared photo albums; host profiles with past events enabling re-invites ("bring your people back").
- **[INFERENCE]** Its growth lever is social proof: seeing who's already going converts undecided invitees; the event page is itself shareable content. Primarily US-oriented and SMS-centric — its fit for Spanish phone habits is unverified.
**Why it matters:** the best-in-class RSVP loop: low-friction join → visible guest list → reminders → post-event artifact (album) that pulls people back.
**[NEST]** For NEST events: show (privacy-safe) attendee context before RSVP, send reminders, and give every event a small afterlife (recap, "say hi to someone you met" prompt). Full guest-list visibility must be adapted for a women-only safety context — first names + photos to verified members only.

### 3.10 Madrid ecosystem (offline competitors = partners)

**ESN Madrid (Erasmus Student Network) — ALIVE.**
- Sources (accessed 2026-07-18): https://esnmadrid.org/ ; https://esn.org/esncard ; https://www.esnuam.org/ ; https://esnucm.org/
- **[FACT]** Federation of university sections (UCM, UAM, UC3M, etc.) welcoming 1,100+ Erasmus students per year in Madrid; paid **ESNcard** (12-month membership) gates events, trips, and hundreds of discounts; **Buddy Program** pairs local students with arriving internationals for the first weeks; heavy welcome-week programming each semester.
- **[INFERENCE]** ESN's engine is the *academic calendar*: demand spikes every September and January. Its weaknesses: mixed-gender mass events, party-leaning culture, and quality that varies by volunteer section.
- **[NEST]** Sync NEST's rhythm to semester arrivals (cohort onboarding, welcome events); consider partnership (verification cross-checks, event cross-promotion) rather than competing with trips.

**Citylife Madrid — ALIVE.**
- Sources (accessed 2026-07-18): https://www.citylifemadrid.com/ ; https://www.citylifemadrid.com/trips/ ; Tripadvisor listing (2026 reviews present).
- **[FACT]** For-profit community for internationals: fixed weekly schedule (salsa Mondays, karaoke Wednesdays, tapas get-together Thursdays), walking tours, trips across Spain/Portugal/Morocco, plus settle-in services (housing help, SIM cards, transport card) and language intercambios.
- **[INFERENCE]** Its retention trick is a *predictable weekly rhythm* — newcomers always know where people will be tonight. Settle-in services create trust before social products do.
- **[NEST]** NEST's city guide should cover arrival logistics ("first two weeks in Madrid" content), and NEST events should adopt a recognizable weekly cadence members can rely on.

**Girl Gone International (GGI) Madrid — ALIVE.**
- Sources (accessed 2026-07-18): https://girlgoneinternational.com/ ; https://girlgoneinternational.com/find-your-ggi/ ; Madrid Facebook group.
- **[FACT]** Volunteer-powered global community for women living abroad; claims 500k+ members across 200+ cities; active Madrid chapter (primarily Facebook-group based); launched "Gone International" trips/retreats arm in 2025. Adjacent: "Las Expateñas" Meetup group for expat women in Madrid (meetup.com/expatwomenmadridconnect/).
- **[INFERENCE]** Proves durable demand for women-only international communities in Madrid — currently served on generic platforms (Facebook, Meetup) with no verification, no matching, and no student focus.
- **[NEST]** This is NEST's clearest validation and gap: same audience need, but NEST adds verification, curation, and a student lens that these communities structurally cannot.

## 4. Cross-cutting findings (research questions)

**4.1 Match → real-world meeting.** The strongest products skip or compress the chat phase: Timeleft/222 book the table before anyone talks; Pie opens a group chat only in service of a scheduled plan; new BFF pairs matching with groups/events. **[INFERENCE]** Chat without a concrete anchor decays in ~3 exchanges (consistent with BFF complaint patterns). **[NEST]** Treat "a plan exists" as the success metric of a match, and make proposing a time/place a one-tap act.

**4.2 Conversation friction.** Verified patterns: Timeleft table conversation cards; Pie auto-opened chats with icebreakers; BFF photo prompts; Peanut audio Pods lowering photo pressure. **[NEST]** Generate icebreaker *suggestions* from shared interests/languages/university context that the user can tap, edit, and send herself — never auto-send, never impersonate.

**4.3 Swipe fatigue.** **[FACT]** ~78% Gen Z dating-app burnout (Forbes Health); Bumble itself moved BFF away from pure swiping. **[NEST]** Small daily curated batches with visible "why her" reasons beat infinite decks; never show an endless queue.

**4.4 Groups/communities.** Geneva → BFF Groups validates interest groups as retention infrastructure; Meetup shows recurring groups outlive one-off events; GGI shows volunteer moderation works but doesn't scale safely. **[NEST]** Start with admin-created, capped, time-boxed circles (approval-gated feature) rather than open user-created communities.

**4.5 Event mechanics.** Best-of-breed loop: capacity + waitlist (Meetup) → no-install RSVP + visible social proof (Partiful) → reminders/text blasts (Partiful) → reveal-style anticipation (Timeleft) → post-event album/afterlife (Partiful) → repeat cadence (Citylife). **[NEST]** NEST already owns admin events + RSVP; the gaps are capacity/waitlist, reminders, attendee social proof, and post-event follow-through.

**4.6 Surfacing mutual interests.** BFF leads with interest matching; Pie/222/Timeleft translate questionnaires into group composition. **[NEST]** NEST's compatibility % is opaque; showing the *reasons* (shared interests, languages, "both new this semester") is the cheapest big win available.

**4.7 Newcomer support.** ESN buddy pairing, Citylife settle-in services, GGI chapters. **[INFERENCE]** Newcomers need logistics + a predictable social calendar in week one, friendships in week three. **[NEST]** Semester-aware onboarding: arrival cohorts, "new here" signals, first-two-weeks guide content.

**4.8 Safety & trust.** **[FACT]** 2025 norms: Bumble ID verification with badge + filter (TechCrunch, 2025-03-17), date-detail sharing with trusted contacts (Bumble "Share Date"; Tinder "Share My Date", April 2024), pre-send harmful-message screening, Peanut selfie-gated womanhood verification. Reporting/blocking is required for user-generated-content apps by Apple's App Review Guidelines (§1.2). **[NEST]** Report + block is table stakes and currently missing — highest-priority backend item; check-ins and trusted-contact sharing are differentiators later.

**4.9 Inactive matches.** **[OPINION]** Ghosted matches are the #1 stated reason women abandon BFF-style apps. Countdown timers (24h) read as dating pressure. **[NEST]** Quietly archive stale chats, offer a low-stakes revive ("send a new question"), recycle expired matches back into discovery, and never shame either side.

**4.10 Personalization & healthy usage.** Quiz-based composition (Timeleft/Pie/222) creates perceived effort-for-me; fixed weekly rituals create healthy, bounded usage (Wednesday dinners, Citylife's weekly schedule) rather than doom-swiping. **[NEST]** Optimize for "one good plan per week," not session length — a defensible wellness story for a women-only brand.

**4.11 What makes products feel "too dating-app."** **[INFERENCE]** from complaint patterns: hot-or-not photo decks, countdown timers, scarcity mechanics ("likes you" paywalls), match-celebration animations, romance-inflected copy ("perfect match"). **[NEST]** The provided language rules (no "soulmate"/"perfect match" etc.) align with the evidence; also avoid *mechanic-level* dating signals, not just words.

**4.12 Moderation & privacy risks observed.** Women-only admission requires a defensible verification process (Peanut's selfie model; NEST's manual review) and a plan for edge cases; event attendance data is sensitive (who was where, when) — Partiful-style public guest lists are not directly transferable; group chats multiply moderation surface (Geneva/Bumble absorbed this cost); monetizing access within community spaces (Meetup) triggers backlash; university affiliation is identifying data for young women and must never be publicly exposed. **[NEST]** Every roadmap item in the roadmap doc carries explicit safety/privacy notes; university stays private; attendance visibility is opt-in and post-RSVP only.

## 5. Complaint roll-up (all **[OPINION]** — anecdotal, but consistent across sources)

| Product | Most repeated complaints | NEST takeaway |
|---|---|---|
| Bumble BFF | Ghosting; matches never convert; chats fizzle ~3 messages in; feels like a dating app; timer pressure | Anchor chats to plans; no timers; reasons-first cards |
| Timeleft | Venue/budget mismatch; no-shows (incl. seated alone); billing/refund friction; chat closed post-event | Price transparency; no-show policy; keep post-event contact open (opt-in) |
| Meetup | Fee hikes; paywalled community features; paid queue-jumping at free events; weak support | Never monetize access or priority inside community spaces |
| Partiful | US/SMS-centric flows; guest-list exposure to strangers (privacy) | EU-friendly flows; attendee visibility only for verified members, opt-in |
| 222 | Uneven group chemistry; variable venue quality | Curation quality is the product; collect table feedback every time |
| Peanut | Life-stage silos can feel exclusionary; community moderation gaps at scale | Keep circles small, admin-moderated, capped |

- **Geneva** — shut down as standalone; lives on inside Bumble BFF Groups (see 3.2).
- **Nextdoor / Facebook Groups** — alive but generic neighborhood/community platforms with no verification story relevant to NEST beyond what GGI already demonstrates.
- **Yubo, Wizz** — teen livestream-social; wrong audience and heavy safety baggage; no useful patterns for a verified women-only product.
